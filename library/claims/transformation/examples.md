# Examples

## Example Concrete Claim

- `For the overlapping migration pages, this pass applied a canonicalize-plus-
  cross-link move to route all cutover-readiness guidance through one canonical
  page while leaving rollout troubleshooting as a separate drill-down topic.`

## Example Supporting Observations

- One page now holds the authoritative cutover-readiness content.
- Neighboring pages were reduced to non-overlapping scope and linked back to the
  canonical page.
- The move preserved separate troubleshooting detail rather than collapsing
  unlike content.

## Example Raw Artifacts

- canonicalization and cross-link receipts
- before and after page excerpts
- updated canonical source register entry

## Example Verdict

- `supported` because the canonical routing move is explicit in both the changed
  pages and the receipt trail.

## Example Open Question Or Follow-Up

- Decide whether the same canonicalization move should be extended to archived
  release notes in a later pass.

## Anti-Patterns

### Common Failures

- The claim says only that content changed, not what conceptual move occurred.
- The evidence treats propagation as part of the move instead of a separate
  claim.
- The move is named after the result (“it’s clearer now”) rather than the
  intervention.
- Multiple transformations are bundled into one thread with no clear boundary.

### Non-Examples

- Bad claim: `We edited the pages to improve clarity.`
- Why it is bad: it does not preserve the methodology-level move.

- Bad observation set: `Several sections were rewritten.`
- Why it is bad: rewriting alone does not show whether the move was
  canonicalizing, tightening, splitting, or something else.
